Metadata for: Diversity, Productivity and Soil Data for North American Grasslands

General Dataset Metadata:

Abstract:

This research program was designed to collect productivity, diversity and soil data in Northern California grasslands. Data collected include species richness, presence absence of plant species, peak standing biomass and nitrogen and phosphorus soil content. The relationship between diversity and productivity can take many different shapes. Soil nutrients can affect species composition, diversity and productivity. This research program will attempt to investigate soil nutrients to as a possible factor in determining the shape of the diversity productivity curve. Funding was used to collect richness, plant presence / absence data, biomass and soil nutrient data.

Geographic Metadata:

 

Geographic Description:

Data were collected in the coastal mountains of Northern California, in the Valley Oak Reserve. The Valley Oak Reserve is adjacent to and managed by Northern Caifornia University (NCU). NCU is located in University Town in Sonoma County, approximately 150 km northeast of San Francisco. The Valley Oak Reserve is located on the east facing slope of the California coastal mountains. The area is primarily oak (Quercus spp) savannah and grasslands. The reserve is 100 km in area.

Methods Metadata:

 

General Sampling Design

♦

Twenty five square1m plots were randomly placed throughout the Valley Oak Reserve. Due to destructive biomass harvest, plots are relocated each year.

♦

Two plastic sample bag (Ziplock) are labeled with the randomly assigned plot number and contents (plant or soil). If more than one bag is needed, all bags are labeled with the contents, plot number and bag number (i.e. the second of four bags of plant clippings from plot 6 will be labeled "Plant, Plot 6, Bag 2/4")

 

Species Presence- Absence

♦

At each plot, one person, starting at the south-east corner of the plot, identifies each plant according to the Jepson manual. Species names are recorded in the field notebook.

 

Species Richness

♦

Species names are used to calculate species richness per plot.

 

Biomass Measurement

♦

All plant material within each 1 m2 plot is clipped at soil level and placed in the sample bag.

♦

Sample bags containing plant matter are brought to the laboratory. If wet, plant matter is dried using paper towels.

♦

Plant material is dried in a drying oven at 80 degrees C for 24 (+/-2) hours.

♦

Plant matter is weighed within 2 hours of drying.

 

Soil Data Measurements

♦

Approximately 0.5 g of soil, free from plant debris, is collected from the middle of the plot.

♦

Soil is placed in appropriate sample bag.

♦

Soil samples are placed into aluminum sample trays and placed into a drying oven and dried at 80 degrees C for 24 (+/- 2) hours.

♦

Soil is ground using a ball mill until powedery and weighed.

♦

Soil sample is analyzed for Phosphorus and Nitrogen using a SoilPro v. 10 machine. All procedures for this machine are followed.

 

Quality Control

♦

All sampling was done by Jane Doe and John Smith.
